Coastal & surf wall art for a warm beach-house feel

Decorating guide · updated 2026

Coastal decor has thankfully moved past the literal "starfish and rope" era. The version that looks good now is retro and stylised — think mid-century travel posters, warm sunsets, a single surfer silhouette — in colours that feel sun-faded rather than nautical-cliché. It suits a real beach house, a surf shack, or simply a sunny living room that wants a holiday mood year-round.

The palette: warm sand meets deep sea

  • Persimmon / warm orange — the sunset hero colour.
  • Deep teal — the ocean counterweight.
  • Sand & cream — the neutral that keeps it airy.
  • Charcoal — for the stylised silhouette and contrast.

This warm-vs-cool pairing is what gives retro surf art its punch. Avoid pure navy-and-white if you want the cosy, vintage feel rather than the crisp coastal one.

Lean into the travel-poster format

Retro travel posters are vertical, full-bleed, with a simple stylised scene and a confident sense of light. They're the easiest way to get the look because one strong print carries a whole wall. A single surfer-at-sunset poster above a console or sofa often does more than a cluster of small frames.

Placement and pairing

Above a sofa, go large or go as a pair — match roughly two-thirds of the sofa width, centre at ~145–150 cm. In an entryway or hallway, a single vertical print at eye level sets a holiday tone the moment you walk in. Coastal art also pairs naturally with natural materials: rattan, light wood, linen.

Great gift angle

Surf and coastal prints make strong, easy gifts for surfers, ocean lovers and anyone with a beach place — specific enough to feel personal, universal enough to be safe. If you're buying for someone, a stylised retro print is a safer bet than a literal photograph of one particular beach.
